# 07 · Brand Identity

## The idea: The Watchful Eye
The movement's identity grows from *The Watchful Eye* — the founding artwork by Ajayi Elijah Snoz: a great eye built from discarded plastics and electronics, with a damaged watch set in its corner. We do **not** reproduce the artwork. We extract its philosophy into a mark that can stand for decades:

> **The planet watches us. We watch over the planet. Observation creates responsibility.**

The eye is the brand's soul because it is simultaneously: an eye (awareness), a planet (the round iris/world), a clock (time running out), and a thing *made of waste* (transformation). One symbol, four meanings, infinitely reducible.

## Brand attributes
Premium · Timeless · Museum-quality · Editorial · Architectural · Minimal · Immersive · Elegant. **Never:** generic-eco, clip-art recycling, green-gradient startup.

## Naming & lockups
- **Movement:** *Because There Is No Planet B* (full, formal). Short forms: *No Planet B* / *#NoPlanetB*.
- **Platform:** *Planet B* (the institution/archive).
- **Tagline tier:** *The living archive of a global movement.*
- Never let "Planet B" imply a second Earth — pair with the Plan-B framing in first use on any new surface.

## Verbal identity
See [02 Manifesto](02-movement-manifesto.md) for voice. Headlines: serif, sparing, declarative. Labels: sans, lowercase or small-caps, calm. Use artists' own words as the primary "copy" of the institution.

## Color & type
Inherits [05 Design System](05-design-system.md): Ink / Paper / Oxblood / Clay, serif display + grotesque text. The oxblood is the brand's only chromatic signature in the chrome.

## Applications (designed to last)
- **Digital:** favicon & social avatar = the eye-mark; OG/share cards = serif title on paper with the mark + chapter line; certificate seal = the eye-mark in oxblood.
- **Institutional:** letterhead, embassy/partner co-branding lockups (Planet B · Royal Norwegian Embassy · Nike Art Gallery), exhibition wall vinyl, catalogue continuity.
- **Future chapters:** the mark stays constant; each chapter gets a city/year wordmark beneath it (e.g. *Planet B · Abuja 2026*), so the network reads as one institution with many homes.

## Co-branding hierarchy
Planet B is the host institution. Partners (Norway, Nike) are credited with equal warmth but visually distinct lockups — Planet B never absorbs or overshadows a partner's own identity, and never the reverse.
