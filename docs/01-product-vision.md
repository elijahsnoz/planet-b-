# 01 · Product Vision

## One sentence
Planet B is the permanent, living archive and connective tissue of a global movement that uses art to make people fall in love with the only planet we have — beginning with the artists, artworks, and people of the inaugural Abuja chapter, and built to hold a hundred chapters and a hundred years.

## What it is — and is not
| Planet B IS | Planet B is NOT |
|---|---|
| A digital institution / cultural archive | An exhibition microsite |
| A registry of people, artworks, and chapters | A portfolio template |
| Infrastructure that outlives any single event | A campaign that ends when the show closes |
| A place that confers permanent identity & provenance | An NFT marketplace |
| A documentary you walk through | A gallery of thumbnails |

## Why it must exist
The Abuja chapter produced something rare: a complete, documented act of cultural and environmental meaning — 15 artists, a sovereign partner (Norway), a legendary host (Nike Art Gallery), a curated exhibition, a panel of environmental leaders, a performance, and a press record. Without an institution to hold it, that becomes a folder of files. With one, it becomes the **Genesis Chapter** of something that researchers cite, museums reference, embassies share, and future chapters inherit.

## Audiences (in priority order)
1. **Future generations & the curious public** — the emotional front door. They must *feel* before they read.
2. **Artists** — current founders (documented as museum subjects) and future applicants who dream of joining.
3. **Researchers, educators, students** — who need trustworthy, citable, structured records.
4. **Institutional partners** — embassies, foundations, galleries, sponsors, governments — who need to share something they're proud of.
5. **Chapter organizers** — who will replicate the model in new cities and need a template + network.

## Success, measured at three horizons
- **Launch (year 1):** The Abuja chapter is fully preserved and navigable; every founder has a permanent profile and a verifiable certificate; partners proudly link to it.
- **Decade:** Multiple chapters exist as peers in one network; the archive is cited in environmental-art scholarship; certificate provenance is independently verifiable.
- **Century:** Planet B is a referenced cultural institution; the data model and preserved media remain readable; the original Genesis Chapter is intact and contextualized, never "archived away."

## Design tenets that govern every decision
1. **Preservation before features.** If a choice risks the longevity or integrity of the record, it loses.
2. **Emotion before information.** Wonder is the recruiting tool; data is the proof.
3. **People are exhibits.** Profiles are curated, not "team cards."
4. **The Genesis Chapter is never old content.** It is the cornerstone, always present-tense.
5. **Earn every animation, every byte.** Museum-grade restraint; 60fps; fast on a phone in Abuja.
6. **Architect for what isn't built yet** — chapters, languages, blockchain — without building it prematurely.

## Non-goals (explicit, to protect focus)
- No commerce / sales of artworks at launch.
- No on-chain tokens at launch (the architecture anticipates them; see [13](13-blockchain-strategy.md)).
- No social feed / user-generated content stream at launch.
- No "green" visual clichés, stock imagery, or recycling iconography ever.
