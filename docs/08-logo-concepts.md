# 08 · Logo Concepts

Three directions, all derived from *The Watchful Eye*. All must work at 16px (favicon) and on a building, in one color (oxblood on paper / paper on ink), and animate as "the eye opening."

> ASCII below is intent, not final geometry — for the design conversation before vector work.

## Concept A — "The Eye-World" (recommended)
A minimal eye whose iris **is** the planet: a perfect circle (iris/Earth) inside an almond eye-line, with a single thin meridian line crossing the iris (longitude → also a clock hand at "almost midnight").
```
        .-‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐-.
     .-'      |        `-.
   (         ( ●)          )      ● = iris/planet
     `-.      |         .-'       | = meridian / clock-hand near 12
        `-‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐‐-'
```
Why: cleanest, most timeless, four meanings in one form. Opens (blinks) on load. Reduces to just the circle+line as an app icon.

## Concept B — "The Assemblage Eye"
The eye-line drawn as a continuous mark, the iris composed of a few abstract fragments (a nod to *made from waste*) that **resolve into a solid circle** on hover/load — literally enacting "waste → whole."
```
      ___________
    /   ◔ ◕ ◑     \      fragments...
    \   →  ●  →    /      ...converging to one iris
      ‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾
```
Why: most conceptually rich and on-message; risk: harder to keep elegant at tiny sizes — reserve the fragment version for large/animated use, ship the solved circle as the static mark.

## Concept C — "The Open Bracket Eye / Lid"
A pure typographic-architectural mark: two thin arcs (upper/lower lid) framing a dot, doubling as a parenthesis around the world — "we hold the planet in view." Most minimal/editorial.
```
   (  ●  )      lids as brackets; ● = planet/pupil
```
Why: most architectural and restrained; pairs beautifully with the serif wordmark; risk: could read as generic without the animation that makes it "blink."

## Wordmark
Serif **PLANET B** with slightly open tracking; the **B**'s counters can subtly echo the eye's curve. Mark sits left of or above the wordmark. Movement lockup stacks the full line *BECAUSE THERE IS NO PLANET B* in small-caps beneath when needed.

## Recommendation
Pursue **Concept A** as the primary mark (timeless, scalable, four-meanings), borrow **Concept B's "converging" animation** as its motion signature, and keep **Concept C** as a possible monogram/loading state. Decide after seeing real vector studies.
