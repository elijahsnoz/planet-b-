# 02 · Movement Manifesto

*This is the voice. Every headline, label, and caption descends from it.*

---

There is no Planet B.

So this — the soil, the water, the air, the city, the single shared sky — is not a draft. It is the only copy.

We are artists. We were handed what the world threw away: bottle caps, broken watches, drink cans, dead electronics, the plastic tide. We did not look away. We looked closer. We picked it up, and we made it look back.

Because the planet is watching us. And we are learning to watch over it. **Observation is where responsibility begins.**

We believe waste is a failure of imagination, not a fact of nature. That what is discarded still has a soul. That *"when the community falls into disrepair, its restoration lies in the hands of those who inhabit it."* That *he who litters the land betrays the land.*

We believe a hand emerging from garbage, with a butterfly on its finger, says more about hope than any statistic.

We are not building a second Earth. We are building humanity's **Plan B** — a plan to keep the first one. Planet B is where that plan is remembered, proven, and continued.

This began in Abuja, on World Environment Day, with fifteen artists, a gallery that has nurtured talent since 1983, and an embassy that crossed an ocean to help. It will not end there.

We keep the names. We keep the work. We keep the proof.

Not as nostalgia — as foundation.

So that a child in 2075 can stand inside this archive and understand exactly how it began, and feel, the way we felt, that it is not too late.

**Because there is no Planet B.**

---

### Voice rules (for writers and the CMS)
- **Plain, certain, warm.** Short sentences. No corporate sustainability jargon ("synergies," "leveraging," "stakeholders" in body copy).
- **Concrete over abstract.** "A damaged watch" beats "themes of temporality."
- **Honor the sources.** Use the artists' own words from their statements wherever possible — quote, don't paraphrase.
- **Present tense for the Genesis Chapter.** It *is*, not *was*.
- **Never preachy.** Show transformation; let the visitor arrive at responsibility themselves.
