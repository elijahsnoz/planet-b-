# 01 · Experience Map

The whole of Planet B as a single world, not a sitemap. Visitors move through **passages**, each a change of state, not a set of pages.

## Passage definitions
- **Threshold** — the sacred arrival. No chrome. Darkness, breath, the Eye. The visitor *realizes* they're entering, rather than being told.
- **Memory** — the *why*. The manifesto, the proverbs, the planet felt (not shown). Slow, monumental type.
- **Discovery** — the *what*. Waste becoming art; the Eye as the lens; artworks that breathe in their frames.
- **Archive** — the *proof*. The Genesis Chapter as a preserved historical record you walk through.
- **Journey** — the *when*. The founding timeline as a descent through 2026 — travelling, not reading.
- **Legacy** — the *who*. Founders and institutions preserved like museum subjects; certificates as permanent identity.
- **Invitation** — the *what now*. Not donate/subscribe — *become part of history*.

## Audiences, one world, many doors
The curious child, the researcher, the embassy, the artist, the journalist — all enter the same Threshold, but the world lets them **find their own depth**: the child stays in wonder; the researcher descends to citable records; the partner finds their chapter. No separate "portals" — one continuous place that rewards however far you go.

## The non-negotiable spine
Every passage advances the **emotional arc** ([02](02-emotional-journey.md)) and the **scroll-as-travel** narrative ([08](08-scroll-narrative.md)). If a surface doesn't move the visitor one emotional step forward, it doesn't belong in the world.
