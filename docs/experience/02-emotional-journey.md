# 02 · Emotional Journey

The experience is engineered as a sequence of **feelings**, in your order. Each beat has a job, a trigger, a craft technique, and an exit feeling. The visitor should never notice the mechanics — only the change inside them.

## The arc
```
ARRIVAL → CURIOSITY → WONDER → SILENCE → REFLECTION → HOPE → RESPONSIBILITY → BELONGING → ACTION
```

| Beat | The job | What's on screen | Craft technique | Exit feeling |
|------|---------|------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Arrival** | Make them stop & quiet down | Near-black void; faint atmosphere; the Eye, closed | Withhold everything. No nav, no copy, ~2s of near-silence. Let the page *breathe* before it speaks. | "Where am I?" |
| **Curiosity** | Pull them in | The Eye opens slowly; one line fades: *Because there is no Planet B.* | A single gesture (the blink) + a single sentence. Scarcity = gravity. | "I want to see." |
| **Wonder** | Awe | Scattered discarded fragments drift and **assemble into an artwork** as they scroll | Scroll-linked transformation (waste→art); the literal movement metaphor | "How is this made of *that*?" |
| **Silence** | Let it land | Full-bleed artwork on deep ground; almost no UI; long pause of whitespace | Restraint. Give the work air. Nothing competes. | Stillness |
| **Reflection** | Turn it inward | An artist's own words, monumental type; a damaged watch ticks once | First-person quotes + the urgency motif | "This is about us." |
| **Hope** | Lift | *Garbage to Grace* — a hand from waste, a butterfly; darkness warms to light | Tonal shift dark→warm; green only as regeneration | "It isn't too late." |
| **Responsibility** | Transfer the weight | The Yoruba proverb; the Eye returns, now watching *them* | The planet watches us — observation → responsibility | "It rests in our hands." |
| **Belonging** | Make it personal | Faces of the founders surface, named; "ordinary people began this" | Human portraits, present-tense | "People like me did this." |
| **Action** | Open a door | The world doesn't end — it becomes a beginning: *the next chapter has not been written* | Quiet invitation, not a sales CTA | "I want to be part of history." |

## Rules of emotional design
- **One feeling per beat.** Never ask for two emotions at once. Whitespace separates feelings the way silence separates musical movements.
- **Earn the turn.** Hope only lands because Silence and Reflection preceded it. Don't shortcut the arc.
- **Withholding is the tool.** The most powerful beats (Arrival, Silence) are mostly *absence*. Resist filling space.
- **The Eye is the emotional anchor** — it opens (curiosity), watches (responsibility), and is present at the start and the turn. It is the through-line of feeling.
- **Reduced-motion keeps the feelings.** Under `prefers-reduced-motion`, the arc survives as a quiet, well-typed vertical essay with cross-fades — still moving, never broken.

## Measuring success (qualitative, but checkable)
For each beat we ask the [four questions](00-README.md): stop-scrolling? museum-proud? child-remembers? historian-preserves? A beat that can't earn all four is cut or remade. The target outcome is a *felt* sentence, not a metric: *"I've never experienced anything like that."*
